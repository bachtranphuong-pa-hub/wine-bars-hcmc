Le Méridien Saigon occupies one of the highest-density 5-star hotel and Grade A office corridors in Ho Chi Minh City, along Tôn Đức Thắng waterfront. A Chinese restaurant concept here faces formidable nearby competition — Crystal Jade Palace at 350m, Kabin at 400m, Dim Tu Tac at 600m, and Michelin-starred Long Triều at 900m. The segment is genuinely contested.

However, strategic analysis reveals two clear, unserved gaps: (1) no competitor on Tôn Đức Thắng offers dedicated VIP/private dining rooms for Chinese cuisine in a 4–12 pax format; and (2) no contemporary Chinese concept with cocktail bar integration exists within 1km. Together, these gaps define a viable and differentiated positioning.

Option A

RISK: MEDIUM
  • 🎯Key differentiator: 3–4 VIP private dining rooms (6–12 pax) — only option on Tôn Đức Thắng
  • 💰Price gap fill: Between Kabin ₫550k ceiling and Long Triều ₫2M floor

  • 👥Target: Riverbank Place office tenants, hotel corporate guests, private celebrations
  • ⏰Hours: L 11:30–14:30 | D 18:00–22:30 | Sat–Sun Yum Cha 10:00–14:30
  • 📊Revenue logic: 13,972m² captive office base = recurring weekday lunch capture
  • ⚠️Requires quality chef team + VIP room fit-out investment

  • 📉Crystal Jade holds Michelin recognition; Kabin holds OAD Top 25 Asia — credibility gap is real
