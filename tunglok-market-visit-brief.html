Shang Palace

1/F Norfolk Mansion, 17–21 Lý Tự Trọng, Quận 1
Since 2007 · Heritage Daily dim sum Hotel-anchored
  • Saigon's longest-running upscale Cantonese institution
  • Extensive dim sum + à la carte; loyal expat & Vietnamese-Chinese base
  • Warm, traditional ambiance — heritage Norfolk Mansion property
  • Observe: Private room setup, service style, lunch volume, customer mix
  • Both delegations present — compare notes post-lunch
Afternoon — Context Note

Why Shang Palace First?

  • 20-yr market anchor — shows how demand was built in HCMC
  • Heritage hotel format = closest Tung Lok analogue in Singapore
  • Loyal Chinese diaspora base: reveals who the core customer truly is
  • Lower price point vs. Long Triều → sets context for the full pricing spectrum
  • Norfolk Mansion's villa setting = note heritage property potential for future site
Day 1 Read: Shang Palace anchors the lower-premium end of hotel Cantonese in HCMC. Its 20-year longevity confirms sustained demand from the Chinese diaspora + expat community. Compare ambiance and service level tonight against Long Triều (Day 2 dinner) to calibrate where Tung Lok fits on the spectrum.

Market Read: HCMC's upscale Chinese dining is dominated by hotel-anchored Cantonese (Shang Palace, Li Bai, Long Triều) — the same model Tung Lok operates in Singapore. The standalone premium format (Dim Tu Tac) captures volume but not margin. White space: A contemporary Cantonese concept in D1 or D3 with strong private dining capacity, modern interior, and a serious dim sum lunch program targeting the corporate segment — currently underserved above Dim Tu Tac but below Long Triều's price ceiling.
