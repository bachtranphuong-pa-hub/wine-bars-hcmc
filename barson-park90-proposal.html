Section 01

The Problem & The Opportunity

⚠ BARSON's Three Structural Challenges

Revenue Dependency

Revenue is disproportionately reliant on in-house hotel groups and LMSG captive guests. No meaningful external traffic; walk-in conversion from the CBD is negligible.

No Distinct Identity

Operating as a standard all-day lobby bar with a generic cocktail/beverage offering. No differentiated reason for the HCMC market to choose BARSON over competing venues in District 1.

Location Constraint

CBD District 1 has limited night-time foot traffic compared to Thao Dien, Binh Thanh, or Le Thanh Ton. Without a compelling destination concept, organic walk-in growth is structurally capped.

The Strategic Question: How does BARSON transform from a captive hotel bar into a self-sustaining destination concept that generates independent brand recognition, attracts external clientele, and creates a defensible competitive position within HCMC's rapidly evolving F&B landscape?

The Partnership — How It Works

🍷 BARSON × Park90 — "Dual-Mode" Fine Wine Bar Model

Lobby bar by day. World-class wine destination by night.

🌅
Before 17:00 — Lobby Bar Mode
BARSON operates as normal — all-day cocktails, beverages, light bites. Standard LMSG service. RCC full operational control. Standard hotel pricing applies.
🌆
After 17:00 — Park90 Fine Wine Mode
Park90 team supplements in: their sommeliers, curated wine presentation, Park90 branding elements. Transforms space into HCMC's only Park90-affiliated fine wine bar.
🍾
SKU Arrangement
Park90 supplies 80% of wine SKUs at wholesale pricing (their SEA aggregator advantage). RCC controls 20% SKUs. Retail price = wholesale + 50–70% mark-up — significantly below standard 2–3× hotel mark-up.
💳
Prepaid Membership (Starbucks-Model)
Members top-up balance (VND 5M / 15M / 50M tiers) → access deeper discounts, exclusive SKUs, members-only events, priority seating. Float creates working capital advantage for RCC.
📍
Location Advantage
Park90 precedent: Regent SG, Conrad SG, InterContinental SG, St. Regis KL, Grand Mayfull Taipei, Setsu Niseko Japan. LMSG as Vietnam debut is a strategic milestone for both parties.
🎓
Knowledge Transfer (Strategic)
Live learning laboratory for RCC's private club ambition. Park90 membership mechanics, wine curation, service standards = direct playbook for future RCC private club development.

Section 04

Strategic Fit & SWOT Analysis

The Bigger Picture: This is not merely a F&B revenue play. It is RCC's first real-world, low-risk experiment in private membership club mechanics — a core strategic competency required for the long-term ambition of building a members-only private club asset within the Royal Canary portfolio.

SWOT Analysis

✅ Strengths

  • LMSG's 5-star platform — instant credibility for Park90 Vietnam debut
  • Michelin-anchored property (AKUNA) elevates F&B perception
  • Existing BARSON infrastructure — minimal fit-out capex needed
  • RCC operational team already on-site
  • Park90's 800+ label portfolio differentiates vs. all HCMC competitors
  • Lowest fine wine pricing in CBD = defensible competitive moat

⚠ Weaknesses

  • BARSON currently has no established external-facing brand
  • CBD location limits organic foot traffic vs. Thao Dien cluster
  • Park90 team integration with LMSG hotel operations requires clear SOP
  • Staffing quality — Park90 sommeliers need to maintain training/standards
  • Wine licensing and import regulations in Vietnam may constrain SKU velocity

🚀 Opportunities

  • First Park90 Vietnam — "exclusive market launch" narrative = PR value
  • Membership float provides non-dilutive working capital
  • Corporate entertainment segment in D1 — untapped wine program
  • Cross-sell to AKUNA guests for post-dinner wine experience
  • Private club pilot: membership model, SKU curation, event programming
  • Marriott Bonvoy integration — global member base potential

⚡ Threats / Risks

  • Park90 term sheet: 80% SKU control creates revenue concentration risk
  • If Park90 relationship ends, concept needs rapid pivot plan
  • HCMC wine market is maturing — competitors will replicate if model succeeds
  • Vietnam import duties and Decree 100 (DUI law) may dampen wine consumption growth
  • Luxury spending slowdown in SEA macro scenario

Implementation Plan & Recommendation

Phased Rollout Timeline

1
Month 1–2 · Commercial Negotiation

Term Sheet Finalization & Legal Review

Negotiate final commercial terms with Park90 (SKU split, fee structure, membership ownership, exclusivity). Engage legal counsel for partnership agreement. Align with Marriott LMSG on operational protocols and branding guidelines. Define P&L responsibility boundaries.

2
Month 2–3 · Setup & Soft Preparation

Space Transformation & Team Integration

Minimal BARSON fit-out (wine display, Coravin stations, lighting mood adjustment, Park90 brand elements). Import license and wine import planning (8–12 week lead time for premium SKUs). Park90 sommelier team training integration with BARSON staff. Membership program design, CRM setup.

3
Month 3–4 · Soft Launch

Invitation-Only Launch — Building Founding Members

Soft launch: invitation-only evening experiences for LMSG VIP guests, AKUNA diners, corporate partners, Marriott Bonvoy Titanium/Ambassador members. Target 50 founding Grand Cru members. Generate media coverage: Park90's Vietnam debut. Collect operational feedback before public opening.

4
Month 4–6 · Public Launch & Programming

Full Operations — Wine Events Calendar

Public launch with PR push (Vietcetera, The Dot, Epicure Vietnam). Monthly winemaker dinners, vertical tastings, Master of Wine sessions. Corporate entertainment packages. Target 200 total members by Month 6. Integrate walk-in wine experience for non-members (access to standard SKU tier).

5
Month 12+ · Review & Private Club Learnings

Model Review — Private Club Strategy Integration

Full-year P&L review. Extract learnings from membership mechanics, SKU curation, event programming. Document SOPs for application to RCC's private club development roadmap. Assess Park90 term renewal vs. RCC-owned independent wine bar model evolution.

Board Recommendation

Approve the Park90 partnership for BARSON and authorize management to proceed to final term sheet negotiation. This is a low-capex, high-strategic-value move that addresses BARSON's core structural weaknesses while simultaneously piloting the membership mechanics central to RCC's private club ambition.
