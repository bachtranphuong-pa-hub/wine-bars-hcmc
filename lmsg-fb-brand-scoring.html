Section 01

Brand Profiles — At a Glance

🔴 Hakkasan

Cantonese Fine Dining
  • Founded London 2001 — Michelin-starred legacy
  • Modern Cantonese: dim sum, Peking duck, luxury seafood
  • Dark, opulent interiors — exclusive nightlife DNA
  • Locations: London, Dubai, Miami, Shanghai, Abu Dhabi
  • Owner: Mohari/Tao Group (2025)
  • Price: $$$$ | Occasion: Special dining
  • Guest appeal: Chinese diaspora, luxury travelers, celebratory groups

🟣 Ling Ling

Asian Infusion · Social Bar
  • Hakkasan Group sub-brand — izakaya-meets-Cantonese
  • Craft cocktails + small plates (Cantonese + Pan-Asian)
  • Party-forward: evolves from dining to drinking to dancing
  • Locations: Dubai (Atlantis), Mexico City, Oslo, Marrakesh
  • Design: Parisian (Gilles & Boissier) — always with view/rooftop
  • Price: $$$ | Occasion: Social, celebration, nightlife
  • Guest appeal: Young affluent, international travelers, scene-setters

🔵 Dishoom

Bombay Comfort Food · All-Day
  • Founded London 2010 — cult following, £300M valuation
  • Bombay Irani café concept: breakfast, lunch, dinner, late drinks
  • Menu: naan rolls, biryani, kebabs, masala chai, cocktails
  • Locations: UK only (11 sites) — LVMH-backed for US expansion 2026
  • Heritage storytelling — nostalgic, immersive design
  • Price: $$$ | Occasion: Casual premium, all-day, groups
  • Guest appeal: British travelers, South Asian diaspora, food tourists

🟢 House of Barbaard

Gentlemen's Barbershop · Cocktail Bar · Boutique
  • Founded 2015, Dutch co-founders — Vietnam-born brand (not a franchise import)
  • Concept: Premium barbershop + signature cocktail lounge + men's boutique in one space
  • 1920s Amsterdam speakeasy aesthetic — "Great Gatsby meets old-school barber"
  • Current locations: Sofitel Saigon Plaza (HCMC) + Meliá Hanoi — hotel-native model
  • All-day: coffee/pastries from 09:00 → cocktails/spirits bar until midnight
  • Bar program: Classic cocktails named after historic figures, imported whisky/spirits
  • Tripadvisor: #156 / 7,741 HCMC — Travellers' Choice (top 10%)
  • Price: $$–$$$ | Occasion: Grooming + social, business lounge, expat hub
  • Guest appeal: Male travelers, expats, business professionals, European guests
  • ⚠️ Note: NOT a restaurant concept — lifestyle/grooming + bar hybrid

⚠️ IMPORTANT — Barbaard is a Different Category:

House of Barbaard is not a restaurant brand — it is a Gentlemen's Lifestyle Concept combining premium barbershop + cocktail lounge + men's boutique. It cannot substitute a full-service F&B restaurant for group dining covers. Its fit should be evaluated as a complementary lounge/lifestyle amenity for LMSG (e.g., replacing or supplementing a lobby lounge or men's spa), not as the primary F&B restaurant partner.
