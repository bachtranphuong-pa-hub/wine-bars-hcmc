Hi [GM] and [F&B Lead],

Hope you're both well. As discussed, we'd like to kick off the F&B strategy work for 3C — excited to do this together and think there's a real opportunity here to move the needle on the address.

Quick context for alignment: the goal is to make 3C Tôn Đức Thắng the go-to F&B address on the street — not just for hotel guests, but for the 1,400+ office workers in the building and the broader Tôn Đức Thắng corridor. We have great bones already with AKUNA's Michelin recognition, but there's more we can do together.

WHAT WE'RE HOPING TO WORK ON TOGETHER

We're thinking this project has three parts — happy to refine the scope once we're aligned:

1. Space audit

A quick stocktake of what we have and what could be activated — current outlets (Latest Recipe, Barson, AKUNA, La Victoire), underutilised spaces (Gaming Center on L3, Pool Bistro level, Royal Club Lounge, function rooms), and any lobby/ground-floor potential we haven't tapped yet.

2. Concept fit framework

Rather than picking concepts ad hoc, it'd be great to agree on a selection lens first. We're thinking across a few angles:

  • Cuisine gap: What's missing in the 1km catchment that we could own?
  • Customer segments: Hotel guests vs. ~1,400 Riverbank Place office workers vs. walk-in from neighbouring hotels — different needs, different dayparts
  • Occasion gaps: Corporate lunch, premium dinner, weekend brunch, late-night bar — which ones are we missing or underperforming?
  • Brand positioning: What concept would most elevate the overall address — not just add revenue, but change the perception of 3C?

3. Operating model options

TWO DIRECTIONS WE'D LIKE YOUR INPUT ON

We've been thinking through two approaches — would love to stress-test these with the team and see which direction makes more sense given your view from operations:

Option A — "One Big Bet"

Bring in one marquee international concept — Michelin-recognised or top Asia-ranked — to anchor the building's F&B identity. The idea is that a high-profile name does the positioning work for the entire address: changes how people think about 3C, drives hotel occupancy, and creates a PR story that's hard to manufacture any other way. Everything else gets calibrated around that anchor.

Next steps: shortlist 2–3 target concepts, run JV feasibility, bring to RCC BOD for LOI approval — target Q3 2026.

Option B — "Build the Ecosystem"

Rather than betting on one name, deliberately build a portfolio that covers every segment and daypart: a premium dinner concept, a strong all-day café/brunch, a proper bar, and a refined private dining offer. Each outlet serves a different guest; together they make 3C the most complete F&B address on the street.

Next steps: finish space audit and gap analysis, write format briefs, run a targeted RFP, pilot one new concept within 6 months.

WHAT WE'RE ASKING FOR

If you're aligned on the direction, we'd love to have the following from the joint team within 60 days:

  1. Space audit — what's available and viable for F&B activation
  2. Competitive gap map — cuisine × price tier × occasion, 1km catchment
  3. Operating model recommendation for each proposed concept
  4. Two strategic options with a business case and clear next steps for each
  5. 3–5 concept candidates per option for RCC BOD review
