BACKGROUND & OBJECTIVE

3C Tôn Đức Thắng sits at the centre of Ho Chi Minh City's highest-density 5-star hotel and Grade A office corridor along the Saigon River waterfront. The building currently houses 305 hotel rooms and 13,972 sqm of Grade A office space (Riverbank Place) — a significant captive customer base that we believe is substantially underserved in terms of premium F&B.

Overarching objective: Establish 3C Tôn Đức Thắng as the top-of-mind F&B destination on Tôn Đức Thắng — relevant across all dayparts, dining occasions, and customer segments — by building a cohesive, differentiated, and commercially excellent F&B ecosystem within the building.

SCOPE OF WORK

We are requesting the joint RCC–Marriott team to undertake an F&B Strategy for 3C Tôn Đức Thắng, covering the following workstreams:

1. Venue & Space Audit

A detailed review of all spaces with current or potential F&B activation, including:

  • Existing outlets: Latest Recipe (Level 3, all-day dining), Barson (bar/lounge), AKUNA (Level 9, Michelin Bib Gourmand), La Victoire (private dining)
  • Underutilised or convertible spaces: Gaming Center (Level 3), Pool/Spa Bistro level, Le Royal Club Lounge (upper floors), Ballroom and function rooms
  • Future potential: Any unactivated ground-floor frontage or lobby space

STRATEGIC PROPOSALS (MINIMUM 2)

Based on the above analysis, we request at least two distinct strategic options, each with: positioning narrative, concept recommendations, preferred operating model, projected revenue contribution, risk factors, and recommended next steps.

To frame the team's thinking, RCC proposes the following two preliminary directions:

Option A — "Anchor & Elevate"

Secure one marquee international concept (Michelin-recognised or top Asia-ranked) as the primary F&B anchor for the building. The anchor concept elevates brand perception of the entire address, drives hotel occupancy, PR value, and F&B revenue simultaneously. All other outlets are calibrated and repositioned around this anchor.

Next steps if selected: Commission F&B concept shortlist and JV feasibility study; initiate brand conversations with 2–3 target operators; present to RCC BOD for LOI approval within Q3 2026.

Option B — "F&B Ecosystem Build"

Rather than a single anchor, develop a deliberate multi-format portfolio that serves all segments and dayparts: one premium dinner concept, one accessible all-day destination, one premium bar/cocktail experience, and one private dining format. Each serves a different segment; the portfolio is greater than the sum of its parts.

Next steps if selected: Complete space audit and gap analysis; develop individual format briefs; issue RFP to selected concept owners; pilot one concept within 6 months.

DELIVERABLES REQUESTED

We request the joint team to deliver the following within 60 days of kick-off meeting:

  1. Space audit report with viable F&B footprint map
  2. Competitive gap analysis (cuisine × price tier × occasion matrix, 1km catchment)
  3. Operating model recommendation per proposed concept
  4. Minimum 2 strategic proposals with full business case and next steps
  5. Shortlist of 3–5 concept candidates per proposal for RCC BOD review
