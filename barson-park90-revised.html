Section 03

Wine Inventory — Consignment "Skin in the Game" Model

Thay vì RCC mua toàn bộ USD 300–500K wine inventory upfront, đề xuất split thành 2 tiers: một phần Park90 cung cấp dạng consignment (bán mới thu tiền), phần còn lại RCC mua outright. Đây là "skin in the game" từ Park90 — nếu wine không bán được, họ chịu một phần rủi ro.

🍾 Proposed Inventory Structure

Tier A — Consignment (Park90)
40–50% of SKUs
~USD 120–150K value
Rare/premium labels (DRC, Petrus, Sassicaia, cult Napa)
Tier B — RCC Purchase (Outright)
50–60% of SKUs
~USD 150–180K value
Entry-premium, BTG wines, high-velocity labels
Total Opening Stock
~300 SKUs, ~1,500 bottles
USD 270–330K total value
RCC cash outlay: USD 150–180K only

🎯 Why "Skin in the Game" Works for Both Parties

  • For RCC: Reduces Day-1 cash outlay from USD 300–500K to USD 150–180K. Rare wines (consignment) are highest risk for slow movement — Park90 absorbs this.
  • For Park90: Consignment labels are their prestige titles — they want them on the list. A Park90 Vietnam without DRC or Petrus is not Park90. Consigning these ensures RCC can list them without capital risk.
  • Aligned incentive: Park90 only gets paid on consignment wines when RCC sells them. They're incentivized to drive activations, member events, and wine education to increase sell-through.
  • Market validation: If premium consignment wines don't sell in 6 months, Park90 takes them back — RCC avoids dead inventory. This is fair market discovery at Park90's risk.
  • Precedent: Consignment wine arrangements are standard in high-end on-trade globally. Park90 already does this with partner restaurants in SG (allocated bottles for member events).

🎯 Final Recommendation — Go Ahead

  • With the revised structure, this deal is now a clear GO. Day-1 risk is VND 6.4B — manageable at management level without special board CAPEX approval. Payback 14 months (base case) is strong for an F&B transformation of this scale.
  • Consignment framing is the most powerful lever. Not only does it reduce RCC's cash outlay — it changes the negotiation dynamic. You're asking Park90 to "put their skin in the game" on their own product. Hard to reject without signaling low confidence in their own wine list.
  • Milestone fee staging creates mutual accountability. Park90 gets paid in full — but only when they deliver. This is the single most important structural change to protect RCC from a Park90 that collects the setup fee and then underperforms on support.
  • The conservative scenario is now viable. Even at low volumes, RCC is cash-positive from Month 1. This removes the "bet-the-farm" risk profile of the original draft terms.
  • Defer Le Cave firmly. Do not let Park90 re-bundle Le Cave into this negotiation. Keep it separate. One deal at a time.
