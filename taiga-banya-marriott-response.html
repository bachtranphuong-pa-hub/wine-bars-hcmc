§4 — Front Office Impact 10 items
#MIHC QuestionTaiga Banya Position
1Spa reservations through hotel system?Taiga manages all reservations via its own PMS. FO staff can make bookings on behalf of in-house guests via a dedicated Taiga booking hotline or embedded web widget within the MIHC internal portal. No dependency on Opera for scheduling.

9Spa desk location & staffing?Taiga reception counter located on Floor 9. Branded collateral display (leaflets, vouchers) at FO lobby level. No Taiga staff stationed at MIHC FO — all queries routed via QR code or dedicated hotline.
10Reporting to FO Manager daily?Yes — daily occupancy and treatment count report delivered to FO Manager by 09:00. Weekly revenue summary. Monthly P&L shared with MIHC GM.

§6 — Engineering Impact 10 items
#MIHC QuestionTaiga Banya Position
1MEP load capacity assessment completed?Taiga will commission an independent MEP assessment prior to fit-out commencement, at Taiga's cost. Full report to be shared with MIHC Engineering within 30 days of lease commencement.
2Equipment loads specifications?Banya/sauna units: estimated 15–25 kW per unit. Wet treatment rooms: additional drainage and sustained hot water demand. Full equipment schedule with electrical load specifications will be provided to MIHC Engineering at fit-out design stage.
3Water consumption forecast?Estimated 20–30 m³/day (banya operations, wet treatment rooms, sauna steam, general spa use). Sub-metered; actual consumption reported to MIHC monthly.
4Hot water usage impact?Significant and sustained demand — banya steam generation and wet treatments require continuous hot water supply. ⚠️ Critical pre-fit-out action: Taiga requests MIHC Engineering to confirm building boiler reserve capacity. If insufficient, Taiga proposes installing a dedicated hot water storage unit — plant room space and capex allocation to be negotiated.
5Drainage requirements?High drainage demand from banya splash areas and wet treatment rooms. Taiga's fit-out contractor will assess existing Floor 9 drainage capacity. If upgrades are required: Taiga bears cost for tenant improvements; structural drainage upgrades to be borne by MIHC as landlord obligation.
6Steam / sauna electrical load?Combined load estimate: ~40–60 kW. Generator/UPS backup required for critical equipment. Request MIHC to confirm whether existing Floor 9 emergency circuit covers this load.
7Preventive maintenance responsibility?Taiga maintains all spa-specific equipment (sauna, steam generators, treatment tables, HVAC units within leased area). MIHC maintains all building systems (main HVAC trunks, primary plumbing lines, main electrical panels).
8Access to plant room?Taiga requests escorted access to plant room with MIHC Engineering representative present. No unescorted access. Key custody remains with MIHC Engineering at all times.
9Energy meter installation?Dedicated sub-meter to be installed by Taiga (at Taiga's cost) prior to operations. Monthly meter readings shared with MIHC Accounting by Day 5 of each month.
10Emergency breakdown protocol?Taiga GM contacts MIHC Engineering Duty Manager via dedicated emergency line. Response SLA: 30-minute acknowledgement; 2-hour on-site response for critical failures (power, HVAC, water supply). A joint Emergency Response Annex to be incorporated into the lease agreement.
💰 Engineering Charges: Electricity → sub-metered at MIHC blended tariff + 5% admin fee  |  Water → sub-metered at municipal rate + 5% admin fee  |  Chiller → m² allocation (Floor 9 leased area / total hotel m²)  |  Engineering standby support → flat monthly retainer (to be negotiated)

#MIHC QuestionTaiga Banya Position
1CCTV integration?Taiga CCTV system operates independently on Floor 9 leased areas. Integration feed to MIHC central security console available upon request. Taiga retains ownership and custody of all footage. Access by MIHC Security upon written request for specific incident investigation only.
2Access to back-of-house?Taiga staff BOH access limited to: Floor 9 leased area, Floor 9 staff corridors, hotel staff cafeteria, and loading dock (for scheduled deliveries). All other BOH areas require MIHC escort.

4Emergency evacuation alignment?Taiga fully integrates into the MIHC Emergency Response Plan. Taiga GM designated as Floor 9 Emergency Warden. Mandatory participation in all hotel-wide fire drills.
5Fire safety certification?All Taiga fit-out works to comply with PCCC (Vietnam fire safety) regulations. Certificate of Compliance to be provided to MIHC before commencement of operations. Taiga maintains own fire safety equipment (extinguishers, emergency lighting) within leased area.
6Medical emergency SOP?First aid kit and AED maintained on Floor 9. All guest-facing Taiga staff trained in basic first aid and CPR. Medical emergency protocol: contact MIHC Security immediately (who contacts 115) + notify MIHC Duty Manager concurrently.
7Data privacy compliance?Taiga complies with Vietnam Decree 13/2023 on Personal Data Protection. Guest data collected by Taiga is not shared with MIHC without explicit guest consent, except where required for billing reconciliation purposes.
8Incident reporting procedure?All incidents occurring on Taiga's leased premises: logged in Taiga's own incident register + MIHC GM notified within 2 hours of occurrence. Written incident report copy provided to MIHC Security within 24 hours.
