§2 — Commercial Structure 8 items
#MIHC QuestionTaiga Banya Position
1Fixed rent / revenue share / hybrid?Hybrid preferred. Base rent (fixed floor) + revenue share above breakeven threshold. Proposed: fixed base + 8–12% GOR above agreed revenue tier. Provides MIHC downside protection while aligning incentives.
2Minimum guarantee?Taiga accepts minimum guarantee tied to ~60% treatment room utilization (projected breakeven). Soft opening waiver requested for first 3 months.
3Rent escalation clause?CPI-linked escalation, capped at 3% per annum. Resist fixed % above CPI — spa revenue growth is usage-dependent.
4Contract term & exit?5-year initial term + 2×3-year renewal options. Exit: 6-month notice; mutual termination for material breach; force majeure carve-out.
5Security deposit?3 months' base rent. Propose phased release: ⅓ returned at Year 2 if no payment defaults.
6Revenue definition?Gross Operating Revenue (GOR) = all spa / fitness / pool / retail billed to guests. Exclude: complimentary treatments, service charge remitted to staff, VAT. Define precisely in Lease Annex.
7Payment cycle?Monthly, within 15 days after month-end. Monthly revenue report to MIHC by Day 10.
8Utilities — sub-metered or allocated?Sub-metered mandatory (electricity & water). Chiller: m² ratio allocation unless sub-metering feasible. Taiga bears sub-meter installation cost.

§6 — Engineering Impact 10 items
#MIHC QuestionTaiga Banya Position
1MEP load capacity assessment?Taiga commissions independent MEP assessment pre-fit-out (at Taiga's cost). Report shared with MIHC Engineering within 30 days of lease commencement.
2Equipment loads?Banya/sauna: 15–25 kW/unit. Wet treatment rooms: additional drainage + hot water demand. Full equipment schedule provided to MIHC Engineering at fit-out design stage.
3Water consumption?Estimated 2–4 m³/day. Sub-metered; monthly report to MIHC.
4Hot water impact?High demand — banya steam and wet treatments require sustained supply. ⚠️ Taiga requests MIHC Engineering to confirm boiler reserve capacity before fit-out. If insufficient: negotiate dedicated unit + capex allocation.
5Drainage requirements?High — banya splash pools + wet rooms. Fit-out contractor to assess Floor 9 drainage capacity. Upgrade cost: Taiga bears if tenant improvement; MIHC bears if structural.
6Steam/sauna load?~40–60 kW combined electrical. Generator backup required — request MIHC to confirm Floor 9 critical load coverage.
7Preventive maintenance?Taiga maintains all spa equipment. MIHC maintains building systems (main HVAC, plumbing trunks, electrical panels).
8Plant room access?Escorted access only (with MIHC Engineering rep). No unescorted access. Key holding: MIHC Engineering.
9Energy meter installation?Dedicated sub-meter mandatory. Taiga covers installation cost. Monthly read shared with MIHC by Day 5.
10Emergency breakdown protocol?Taiga GM contacts MIHC Engineering Duty Manager via dedicated line. SLA: 30-min acknowledgement / 2h on-site for critical (HVAC, water, power). Joint Emergency Response Annex proposed in lease.
💰 Engineering Charges: Electricity → sub-metered, MIHC blended tariff + 5% admin  |  Water → sub-metered, municipal rate + 5% admin  |  Chiller → m² allocation  |  Engineering standby → flat monthly retainer (to be negotiated)
