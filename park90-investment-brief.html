01 · Business Model
How Park 90 Works
Park 90 operates as a premium wine & beverage bar at Le Méridien Saigon, capturing hotel guests as a primary source. The model assumes conservative ramp-up from a low base — reflecting Barson's current low foot traffic — growing from 5% to 25% incremental hotel guest contribution over 24 months.

Key Model Assumptions (Conservative Baseline)

  • Revenue model: Beverage-only (100% bev, 0% food) · Avg spend 1,050K VND/guest (~$40 USD)
  • Guest source: Incremental hotel guests only — starts at 5% of Barson guests (t=1), ramps to 25% by t=25
  • Critical risk: Barson currently has low occupancy → 25% of a low base = still low absolute guest count
  • Investment: 12.73B VND initial (t=0) + 3.975B VND additional (t=3) = 16.7B total
  • D&A: 145.9M VND/month (straight-line over model period)
  • Fixed opex: Wages 80M + Marketing 50M + License 65.5M/month = ~195.5M fixed base

04 · Breakeven Analysis

⚠️ Core Issue: Low Base Problem

  • Model assumes Barson hotel guests as source — but Barson is currently low-traffic
  • Growing from 5% to 25% of a small base → absolute guest count remains modest (147 → 1,085/month)
  • At 1,085 guests/month × 1,050K VND = 1.14B revenue — strong unit economics but volume-constrained
  • Solution: expand guest source beyond hotel guests (external walk-in, events, memberships)
  • If same unit economics apply to 2,000 external guests/month → revenue doubles, EBITDA scales rapidly
05 · Revenue Enhancement Proposals
Closing the Gap — 6 Initiatives
The conservative model is constrained by a single guest source (hotel occupancy). The following initiatives can materially improve revenue without additional CAPEX, by expanding reach, increasing frequency, and adding high-margin revenue streams.
🍷
Wine Membership Program
HIGH IMPACT · Low CAPEX
Launch 3-tier membership (Silver/Gold/Diamond) with monthly bottle allocation, priority reservation, and wine cellar access. Lock in recurring revenue independent of hotel occupancy.
Est. +150–300M VND/month at 50–100 members
🎉
Private Events & Buy-outs
HIGH IMPACT · Immediate
Position Park 90 for corporate wine dinners, birthday buyouts, and brand activations. Leverage hotel banquet team for F&B coordination. Min spend 20M VND/event.
Est. +200–400M VND/month at 4–8 events
🌐
External Walk-in Traffic
MEDIUM · Requires marketing
Target external HCMC premium consumers, not just hotel guests. Social media presence, Google listing, collaborations with premium lifestyle communities (67 Pall Mall, wine clubs).
Est. +300–600M VND/month if 300 external guests
🍽️
Add Food Component
HIGH IMPACT · Requires kitchen
Current model has 0% food. Adding curated wine-pairing snacks / charcuterie boards / shared plates can increase average spend by 30–50% without full kitchen. Leverage hotel kitchen for prep.
Est. +30–50% APC uplift → +200–400M/month
📦
Wine Retail & Take-home
MEDIUM · Low effort
Offer curated take-home bottles at retail markup. Barson as distributor advantage: lower cost, higher margin. Display as cellar recommendation — converts tasting into purchase.
Est. +80–150M VND/month at 2–3 bottles/guest conversion
📅
Wine Education & Masterclasses
MEDIUM · Brand builder
Monthly WSET-level tasting events, winemaker dinners, vertical tasting series. Ticket price 500K–2M VND/person. Builds community, creates PR, attracts high-spend repeat guests.
Est. +60–120M VND/month · 2 events × 30 guests

Lease Model Mechanics

  • Park90 pays RCC/Hotel a monthly lease (fixed + % revenue) for Barson space
  • RCC recovers space cost regardless of Park90 performance
  • Park90 reduces equity at risk → more motivated to drive volume
  • Structure: e.g. 100M VND/month base + 8% of revenue above 600M
