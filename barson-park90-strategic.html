Section 02

Pros & Cons — Full Analysis

✅ Pros

  • Proven model: Park90 operates in Regent SG, Conrad SG, InterContinental SG, St. Regis KL, Grand Mayfull Taipei, Setsu Niseko — track record across 5 markets
  • Pricing moat: SEA aggregator wholesale → 50–70% markup vs. hotel standard 2–3×. Guest sees better prices, RCC keeps margin
  • 800+ label portfolio: DRC, Petrus, Screaming Eagle by the glass via Coravin — impossible for standalone bars to replicate
  • Award-winning sommelier team: Mason Ng (Best Sommelier Asia-Oceania 2023), Celine Jung (Best Sommelier SG 2023) — instant credibility transfer
  • Vietnam debut exclusivity: First Park90 in Vietnam = PR value, Marriott Bonvoy integration, media coverage
  • Membership model mechanics: Prepaid float, annual renewal, behavioral lock-in — direct learning lab for RCC's private club ambition
  • 4× annual activations: Winemaker visits, MW masterclasses — drives repeat traffic and PR independently
  • 3-year term + renewal: Long enough to build membership base, short enough for RCC exit if underperforming
  • Reciprocal benefits: BARSON members get access to Park90 SG/KL/Taipei — premium perk for Grand Cru tier

✗ Cons & Concerns

  • USD 250K setup fee is steep: Covers consultancy, design, training, launch PR — but non-refundable and upfront. High sunk cost if execution fails.
  • USD 300–500K wine inventory: Working capital tied in illiquid asset (wine). Sell-through risk if membership ramp is slow.
  • Venue conflict: Park90 wants privacy of shop-lot/villa. LMSG lobby bar is semi-public. This fundamentally changes the experience and member demographic.
  • BARSON baseline is event-dependent: 30% of March revenue came from 3 event days. Organic floor is only ~39M/day. Park90 alone may not compensate for soft days.
  • Vietnam wine market is small: Only 0.8% of national alcohol consumption. Wine culture still nascent outside expat/UHNWI segment.
  • Import duty burden: Vietnam import tax + SCT adds 30–50% vs. Singapore. Competitive pricing advantage of Park90 is partially offset.
  • Park90 controls 70% SKU: RCC limited in curating its own wine identity. Cross-promotion constraint.
  • Certified sommelier scarcity: Vietnam has very few CMS/WSET L4 certified sommeliers. Quality staff will be hard and expensive to hire.
  • Membership culture not established: Vietnam F&B membership is rare. Converting guests to prepaid members requires significant trust-building time (2–3 years per Park90 own analysis).
  • Le Cave retail risk: Additional USD 500K capital for retail arm — separate bet on e-commerce wine retail in Vietnam, unproven market.

Section 04

Negotiation Playbook — Term by Term

🔴 Setup Fee — Top Priority
Draft: USD 250,000 one-off, upfront
Target: USD 150,000 upfront + USD 100K deferred to Year 2 performance milestone (revenue > USD 1M/yr)
Tactic: Frame as "Vietnam market pioneer risk premium" — Park90 needs RCC as much as RCC needs Park90 for Vietnam debut. Offer to accelerate opening (Nov 2026) in exchange for fee reduction. Anchor on KL/Taipei precedent — ask what setup fee was there.
🔴 Wine Inventory Phasing
Draft: USD 300–500K upfront before opening, full payment or 30–60 day credit
Target: USD 120–150K opening stock → quarterly replenishment based on sell-through. USD 300K total cap in Year 1.
Tactic: Use Vietnam import logistics as justification — wine takes 8–12 weeks to clear customs. Cannot over-order without sell-through data. Request quarterly review clause to adjust SKU mix based on actual guest demand. Point to KL St. Regis opening model — presumably similar phased approach.
🟡 Annual License — Ramp Protection
Draft: 2.5% GTO, minimum USD 30,000/yr (USD 2,500/mth)
Target: 2.5% GTO, minimum USD 15,000/yr Year 1 + USD 20,000 Year 2 + USD 30,000 Year 3+
Tactic: "Minimum applies below USD 100K/mth revenue — that takes 12–18 months to achieve in a new market." Graduated minimum protects both parties during ramp-up. Park90 still earns growing fees as revenue builds. Frame as aligned interest, not cost-cutting.
🟡 SKU Control — RCC Identity
Draft: Park90 right to supply minimum 70% SKUs
Target: Accept 70% Park90 — but RCC retains 100% pricing authority on the 30% independent SKUs. RCC's 30% to include: Vietnamese wine, local spirits, curated Asian labels — distinct identity.
Tactic: Not about reducing Park90's share — about ensuring RCC has an independent narrative ("our curation" vs "Park90's curation"). Also enables RCC to develop its own wine buying relationships for future standalone ventures.
🔴 Venue — Must Clarify First
Draft: "Preference for independent shop-lot / private villa for privacy of members and guests"
Target: Either Park90 accepts LMSG lobby with dedicated private section — OR explore LMSG Riverbank Place / separate D1 shop-lot as venue alternative.
Tactic: First, ASK — is this a hard requirement or soft preference? If soft: propose a dedicated private section of BARSON (semi-enclosed, separate entrance after 17:00, private signage). If hard: propose Riverbank Place (within LMSG complex, more private). Park90's KL location is St. Regis Drawing Room — semi-private, within hotel. Precedent supports hotel venues.
🔴 Membership Ownership — Non-Negotiable
Draft: Not clearly defined — ambiguous
Target: RCC owns 100% of: member data, CRM, renewal relationship, membership float (prepaid funds). Park90 has read-access for reciprocal benefits only.
Tactic: This is a data and financial asset issue. Membership float = RCC working capital. If Park90 co-owns, they could theoretically claim on the float or member list if partnership ends. Must be explicit in agreement: "Member data is sole property of Licensee. Park90 may not contact members directly without Licensee consent."
🟡 Vietnam Exclusivity
Draft: Not mentioned
Target: RCC is exclusive Park90 licensee in Vietnam for 3 years. First right of refusal on any additional Vietnam outlet. Park90 cannot license to competitors.
Tactic: This is high-value for RCC, low-cost for Park90 (they're new to VN anyway). Frame as "we're investing heavily in your Vietnam launch — we need protection." Offer to extend initial term to 5 years in exchange for exclusivity.
🟡 Exit Protection for RCC
Draft: 3-year term, 6-month notice for renewal
Target: Add: RCC right to terminate after Year 1 if revenue < threshold (e.g., < VND 1.5B/mth for 3 consecutive months). Recovery of pro-rated setup fee if Park90 materially underperforms its obligations.
Tactic: Not asking for general exit right — asking for performance-based exit triggered only if targets badly missed. This is fair and Park90 should accept if confident in model. Frame as "aligned incentive — you only want us if we're succeeding."
🟢 Le Cave Retail — Defer Explicitly
Draft: Presented as parallel launch, same timeline
Target: Exclude Le Cave from Phase 1 agreement entirely. Explicit language: "Le Cave de Park90 Vietnam to be separately negotiated no earlier than 18 months after Park90 @ Saigon opening."
Tactic: Le Cave adds USD 500K inventory + USD 18K annual license + retail operations complexity. Cannot execute both simultaneously. Park90 may push back — frame as "focus on quality of launch, not quantity of venues." Offer La Cave as Year 2 milestone reward for beating Year 1 targets.
🟢 Opening Timeline — Prefer Feb 2027
Draft: Either End-Nov 2026 or End-Feb 2027
Target: End-Feb 2027 (post-CNY). Use the extra time productively.
Tactic: Feb 2027 gives: (1) time to import first wine shipment properly, (2) negotiate/finalize venue, (3) hire + train sommelier team, (4) build pre-launch membership waitlist. Nov 2026 is only 7 months away — too tight given CAPEX negotiation and import logistics. Post-CNY also means domestic UHNWI are back from holiday, higher opening-night attendance.

Section 07

Final Recommendation for Anh Quang

🎯 Strategic Recommendation — 5 Points

  • This is a GO — conditional. Park90's model is proven, the market gap is real, and the strategic learning value for RCC's private club ambition is significant. But it must be done on negotiated terms, not draft terms.
  • Venue question first, everything else second. Before spending any more time on financials, send one email to Park90: "We're interested in LMSG lobby bar format (dedicated section, private after 17:00). Is this compatible with your requirements?" Their answer determines the entire deal structure.
  • Negotiate setup fee from USD 250K to USD 150K + USD 100K deferred. This is the single biggest lever. Reduces Year 1 CAPEX from VND 14B to ~VND 9B — changes payback from 5 years to ~2.5 years. Park90 should accept given they need a Vietnam anchor partner.
  • Phase wine inventory: USD 150K at opening, quarterly replenishment. This is about cash flow management, not commitment. Park90 still gets their sales — RCC just doesn't over-commit to illiquid stock before demand is proven.
  • Defer Le Cave explicitly. This is non-negotiable for Phase 1. Retail wine e-commerce in Vietnam is a separate, complex business that requires separate licensing, separate management, and separate capital. Bundling it with the bar launch is a recipe for execution failure. Park90 should understand — quality of launch matters more than speed of expansion.
